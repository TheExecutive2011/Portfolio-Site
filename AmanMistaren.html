Chronological Backstory

Childhood and Upbringing in Shadowfell

Aman Mistaren was born into poverty on Briarborne Isle, one of the five islets that make up the Shattered Isles. The Shattered Isles are just off the mainland city of Gloomwrought, the largest city in all of Shadowfell. Aman was the oldest of three siblings, born to parents who struggled to make ends meet. Rennin was his younger brother, with the youngest of the three siblings being his sister Verra.


Aman's parents were both laborers who worked long hours to support the family. His father worked long hours on the docks of Gloomwrought, while his mother took odd jobs wherever she could. Despite their hardships, they were loving parents who did their best to shield their children from the dangers and corruption of the city. They instilled in their children the values of hard work, perseverance, and compassion, and always stressed the importance of family.


Aman spent his early years playing in the streets with other children from the neighborhood and exploring the islet's rocky beaches. One of his friends was a boy named Bob. Aman and Bob connected from the moment that they were first introduced, quickly becoming best friends. Bob was an outcast, though, mostly because he had a weird name. When other children would make fun of Bob for his weird name, though, Aman would come to his defense. The two of them would become lifelong friends, sharing a sense of adventure and mischief that would lead them on all sorts of unexpected adventures.


Aman had a close relationship with his grandparents who lived in a small cottage nearby, on the outskirts of the city. His grandfather was a retired factory worker, with his grandmother being a retired chef. He would frequently eat the delicious food that she cooked, particularly when there wasn't enough food at home. He often visited them to listen to their stories of the olden days. Frequently, his grandfather would tell stories of magical sorcerers and dragons that just seemed to be too fantastical to even possibly be real, but Aman always held a special appreciation for this time spent with his grandfather.


One day while he was roaming the streets with Bob, they encountered a boy their age who was bullying a group of younger children. Without a thought, Bob cast invisibility on himself, disappearing from sight entirely. A bucket that was sitting on the ground suddenly began to float, eventually landing upside down on the bully's head. Shortly after, the bully found himself flat on his back with a trash can emptied on top of him. The younger children all scattered, giggling as they did. Aman felt something grab his arm and tug him in the direction of a nearby alleyway. Once hidden, Bob reappeared in front of him. Both boys were grinning from ear to ear, quite pleased with the events that just took place. Aman asked Bob where he learned that, to which Bob replied that his parents taught him.


Upon hearing that, Aman felt a bit deflated. His parents had repeatedly forbidden him from ever pursuing any knowledge of magic. They said that it would only lead to him ruining his life. This was their stance, though Aman suspected that they knew and used magic themselves. He saw little things happen that couldn't otherwise be explained. Broken things that were suddenly mended, injuries that were immediately healed, and once even a hand that seemed to float in the air while assisting them with chores. Aman did not know what was really happening with his parents or why they so strictly forbade him from learning about magic, but one thing was certain - he knew he wanted to learn about it.


Aman asked Bob to teach him, but Bob replied that it wasn't possible for just anyone to learn magic. He said that he was born with the ability and so were his parents. Aman insisted, so Bob caved and taught him how to cast the invisibility spell that he had just used. Aman repeated what Bob had taught him, suddenly disappearing from sight. Bob gasped, shocked at what just happened. The boys had just discovered that Aman was also able to learn magic.


Over time, Bob would continue learning from his parents. As he did, he would teach Aman what he learned while also letting Aman borrow old tomes to further his studies. Aman often experimented with casting spells in secret. His siblings would sometimes catch him muttering strange incantations under his breath, but he always managed to convince them that he was just reciting a poem or practicing a monologue for a play.


As Aman grew older, he began to open up about his studies to his grandfather. He trusted his grandfather, frequently going to him for advice on topics that he would never open up to his parents about. From the moment the topic came up, his grandfather perked up and was clearly excited that Aman had discovered his abilities.


Aman's grandfather revealed that he, too, had such abilities. In fact, his mother did, as well. These abilities had been genetically passed down generation after generation, with Aman having inherited these abilities from an ancient bloodline of shadow sorcerers.


He then explained that Aman's mother - without explanation - refused to teach Aman about these inherited abilities. He still doesn't know why, but has respected her wishes. Despite her wishes, Aman could not resist the pull of his innate magical abilities and would continue to secretly practice his magic in the shadows. His grandfather assisted him, while Bob continued teaching what he knew, as well. Over time, Aman would hone his skills as a shadow sorcerer. He would become quite powerful, having mastered numerous skills beyond what would have been expected of him at his age.



Pursuit of Change in Shadowfell

As Aman grew older, he became increasingly aware of the stark socioeconomic inequality and corruption that plagued Shadowfell, and he began to question the ruling class that held power over the common people. The rich grew richer while the poor struggled to make ends meet, often resorting to desperate measures to survive. Aman could not bear to see his neighbors, friends, and family suffer under this oppressive rule, and was determined to do something about the problem.


Aman began to devise various plans to undermine the ruling class and level the playing field for everyone. He worked tirelessly to gather like-minded individuals who shared his vision of a fair and just society. Together, they plotted and schemed, but their efforts were repeatedly met with failure. Their attempts to undermine the ruling class were met with resistance at every turn. Equipment that was stolen was quickly replaced. Misinformation was promptly corrected. Any attempts to infiltrate their inner circle were blocked. Every act that Aman and his friends attempted to carry out was entirely ineffective. The ruling class had vast resources at their disposal and would stop at nothing to maintain their power and wealth. Aman and his allies were often outmatched and outmaneuvered, and they suffered numerous setbacks along the way. Despite the many setbacks, Aman formed close bonds with several of people that he made these efforts with. He found solace and companionship in their company, making it easier to deal with the repeated failures.


In an effort to gain an edge on his enemies, Aman continued to hone his skills as a shadow sorcerer, becoming more powerful with each passing day. He studied ancient tomes and sought out wise mentors who could guide him in his quest for knowledge and mastery. Aman's rapid mastery of shadow magic allowed him to become a formidable opponent to the ruling class, but he still struggled to overcome the immense power they wielded. He knew that he needed to become even stronger if he was ever to succeed in his mission.


In the meantime, Aman continued his attempts to be a thorn in his enemies’ sides. His latest plan involved a daring, bold plan that would surely undermine the ruling class and expose their corruption. He worked closely with a group of other childhood friends that he had grown up with, trusting them implicitly to keep his plan a secret. However, one of the people involved, Lenas, proved to be a traitor. He had been swayed by the promises of the ruling class, and he betrayed Aman and his allies, leading to the complete failure of their plan. Aman was captured and badly beaten, then promptly thrown into the streets. Aman had put his faith in Lenas, and his betrayal left him shaken and disillusioned. He began question the loyalties of everyone else that he worked with, as well as whether he would ever be able to bring about the change he so desperately sought.


Aman's quest for justice and equality had taken a toll on him, both physically and emotionally. He had been beaten down time and time again, and he began to feel that his efforts were entirely in vain. Aman had taken a dangerous risk that nearly cost him his life. He was left alone, battered, and broken. As he continued to ponder his current situation, he realized that his mission in Shadowfell was impossible to achieve. The ruling class was too powerful, and Aman realized that he would never be able to bring about the change he so desperately sought for the people of Shadowfell.



The Legend

Even as Aman grew into adulthood, his grandfather continued to tell him stories of old legends and fables. Among these was a tale of a powerful artifact that would grant unstoppable magical abilities and strength to its wielder. The artifact was used to overthrow an oppressive ruler, not so unlike that of the current regime. Aman dismissed this as just another one of his grandfather's fantastical stories that he so adored.


Years later, while reading an ancient tome he stumbled upon during his studies of shadow magic, Aman came across a passage describing the same artifact that his grandfather once told a legend about. He was stunned and in disbelief. Could it be true that such a powerful artifact exists? Aman became obsessed with the idea of finding this artifact, knowing that it could give him the power he needed to finally bring about the change he sought for Shadowfell. For years, Aman searched in secret for any information about the artifact's location, gathering every clue and lead he could find. His search was long and arduous, but he never gave up. Eventually, he felt that he was getting closer to his goal, but he was hesitant to trust anyone with this information.


One day, Aman finally broke his silence and shared his discovery Bob, who had continued to be Aman's closest friend. Bob was somewhat surprised that Aman had discovered information about the artifact, but not entirely. He had heard legends of the same artifact from his own family. Between the legends that Aman's grandfather passed down and the ones passed down to Bob, they were able to piece together enough details for the legends to make sense in the context of what the tome was saying about it.


Aman explained to Bob the importance of the artifact in his persistent, but frustrating, dream to eliminate the socioeconomic inequality and corruption that pervaded Shadowfell. The artifact was the key to his success where he had repeatedly failed before. Aman's newfound hope and inspiration renewed his determination to find the artifact, no matter the cost.


Aman and Bob worked tirelessly together to continue the search for the artifact, despite facing numerous obstacles and setbacks. They knew that the journey would be dangerous and treacherous, but they were both determined to see it through to the end.



Finding the Artifact

Aman and Bob journeyed for weeks through treacherous terrain, following the clues and rumors that they had pieced together about the artifact's location. They eventually arrived at what was supposed to be the mouth of a cave. What they found instead was a mountainside that was covered in rocks and boulders. After searching around, they determined that this looked like a rockslide had occurred a long, long time ago. Moving some of the rocks revealed what looked to be a cave entrance - right where they initially thought it should be located.


After stepping into the cave, they noticed that it was incredibly well-preserved. As they ventured deeper into the cave, they discovered evidence of past habitation by someone who had once lived there long ago. Exploring further revealed structures that had been built into the cave - structures that very much resembled a dwelling.


Aman and Bob pressed on, driven by their determination to find the artifact. They eventually came upon a large chamber of the apparent dwelling, where the artifact openly sat on a table, looking exactly as the legends had said and the tome had depicted. Aman approached the table, feeling a sense of anticipation mixed with trepidation. Both of them knew what the legends said of the artifact. Anyone who touched it was to gain unstoppable magical abilities and strength. Aman looked back at Bob, who anxiously encouraged him to pick it up.


As he reached for the artifact, he felt a surge of energy coursing through his body, and a portal opened before him. The portal shimmered with otherworldly light, and Aman felt a powerful force pulling him towards it. He hesitated for a moment, unsure of what lay beyond the portal, but his thirst for power and his desire for change ultimately drove him forward.


Aman stepped through the portal, but the moment that he did it closed behind him. He found himself in a strange and unfamiliar place. He was alone, with no sign of Bob anywhere. Most importantly, he did not feel any different than before. If he was provided with unstoppable magical abilities and strength, he certainly wasn't aware of it. He was disoriented and confused, but he knew that he had to press on. He had come too far and sacrificed too much to give up now. He needed to find the source of the legendary powers. He picked himself up and began to explore his surroundings, searching for any clues that might help him figure out where he was



Arrival in the Material Plane

Aman looked around, still clutching the artifact that had led him to this strange and unfamiliar world. The artifact seemed to do nothing anymore - no longer showing any signs of magic, and certainly not any signs of providing the unstoppable magic abilities and strength that he convinced it would provide. He felt a sense of profound disappointment and anger wash over him. He had sacrificed so much in his quest to obtain the artifact, only to be left empty-handed once again.


As he took in his surroundings, Aman realized that he was in a new world entirely distinct from his own. The skies were a different color, the vegetation was unfamiliar, and the air smelled strange. The land he was in seemed almost mythical, but it was one that he certainly recognized from his studies - the Material Plane. He knew that Shadowfell existed in a realm that was separate from others. He knew that others had traveled between those realms before. He did not, however, think that he would ever get the opportunity to observe this land for himself.


Looking back at the artifact, Aman observed that it had strange writings etched into it, but Aman could not understand what they meant. He was fluent in Common - a necessity to effectively communicate with the many races that called the Shattered Isles home. He also knew Abyssal, as that was required for his studies of magical summoning. The writings etched into the artifact, though, looked nothing even relatively similar to either language he was familiar with. What if he had done something wrong? What if there was still more to do? He began to feel lost and disoriented, unsure of what to do next.


After some exploration and thought, Aman determined that the legends he had been told were as incorrect as he initially believed when his grandfather told him those fantastical stories. He was now convinced that the artifact was a simple portal to this strange new world. He was both frustrated and curious at the same time. Frustrated that he had failed to gain the power he had hoped for, but curious about what this new world had to offer. Aman began to think through his current situation, determining that he should explore this plane of existence that he found himself in. After all, it still might hold something of value for him in his quest to overthrow the ruling class back home. He thought that there would certainly be something to learn from the beings in this plane that might assist him in his endeavors. Moreover, this excursion could prove to be one that provides him a welcome break from his struggles and repeated failures back home.


Aman decided that he must keep his true identity hidden until the time was right, though. He did not know what allies the powerful elites back home might have, and certainly did not know if they had any connection to the other planes. He felt a sense of relief knowing that he could explore this new world without fear of being easily discovered by his enemies, but did not want to compromise that by sharing his real name. While in this plane, he determined that he would go by the name Merrick Ravenfall. With “Merrick” meaning “fame” or “power”, it seemed like an appropriate reminder of his purpose here in this plane. As he explored further, he began to wonder if he would ever be able to improve the lives of the poor and oppressed in Shadowfell. The daunting task of toppling the ruling class seemed more impossible than ever before.


Nevertheless, Aman was determined to press on. He knew that he had a long journey ahead of him, but he also knew that he had to keep moving forward. He had come too far and sacrificed too much to give up now. As he continued his journey, Aman was certain that he would discover new skills and make new allies that would eventually lead him back to Shadowfell, where he would continue his quest for justice and equality.




Spells

Cantrips

Mage Hand

A spectral, floating hand appears at a point you choose within range. The hand lasts for the duration or until you dismiss it as an action. The hand vanishes if it is ever more than 30 feet away from you or if you cast this spell again.


You can use your action to control the hand. You can use the hand to manipulate an object, open an unlocked door or container, stow or retrieve an item from an open container, or pour the contents out of a vial. You can move the hand up to 30 feet each time you use it.


The hand can't attack, activate magic items, or carry more than 10 pounds.


Mind Sliver

You drive a disorienting spike of psychic energy into the mind of one creature you can see within range. The target must succeed on an Intelligence saving throw or take 1d6 psychic damage and subtract 1d4 from the next saving throw it makes before the end of your next turn.


This spell’s damage increases by 1d6 when you reach certain levels: 5th level (2d6), 11th level (3d6), and 17th level (4d6).


Minor Illusion

You create a sound or an image of an object within range that lasts for the duration. The illusion also ends if you dismiss it as an action or cast this spell again.


If you create a sound, its volume can range from a whisper to a scream. It can be your voice, someone else's voice, a lion's roar, a beating of drums, or any other sound you choose. The sound continues unabated throughout the duration, or you can make discrete sounds at different times before the spell ends.


If you create an image of an object--such as a chair, muddy footprints, or a small chest--it must be no larger than a 5-foot cube. The image can't create sound, light, smell, or any other sensory effect. Physical interaction with the image reveals it to be an illusion, because things can pass through it.


If a creature uses its action to examine the sound or image, the creature can determine that it is an illusion with a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC. If a creature discerns the illusion for what it is, the illusion becomes faint to the creature.


Prestidigitation

This spell is a minor magical trick that novice spellcasters use for practice. You create one of the following magical effects within range:


  • You create an instantaneous, harmless sensory effect, such as a shower of sparks, a puff of wind, faint musical notes, or an odd odor.
  • You instantaneously light or snuff out a candle, a torch, or a small campfire.
  • You instantaneously clean or soil an object no larger than 1 cubic foot.
  • You chill, warm, or flavor up to 1 cubic foot of nonliving material for 1 hour.
  • You make a color, a small mark, or a symbol appear on an object or a surface for 1 hour.
  • You create a nonmagical trinket or an illusory image that can fit in your hand and that lasts until the end of your next turn.

If you cast this spell multiple times, you can have up to three of its non-instantaneous effects active at a time, and you can dismiss such an effect as an action.


Chill Touch

You create a ghostly, skeletal hand in the space of a creature within range. Make a ranged spell attack against the creature to assail it with the chill of the grave. On a hit, the target takes 1d8 necrotic damage, and it can't regain hit points until the start of your next turn. Until then, the hand clings to the target.


If you hit an undead target, it also has disadvantage on attack rolls against you until the end of your next turn.


This spell's damage increases by 1d8 when you reach 5th level (2d8), 11th level (3d8), and 17th level (4d8).


1st Level

Feather Fall

Choose up to five falling creatures within range. A falling creature's rate of descent slows to 60 feet per round until the spell ends. If the creature lands before the spell ends, it takes no falling damage and can land on its feet, and the spell ends for that creature.


Magic Missile

You create three glowing darts of magical force. Each dart hits a creature of your choice that you can see within range. A dart deals 1d4 + 1 force damage to its target. The darts all strike simultaneously, and you can direct them to hit one creature or several.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher, the spell creates one more dart for each slot level above 1st.


Disguise Self

You make yourself--including your clothing, armor, weapons, and other belongings on your person--look different until the spell ends or until you use your action to dismiss it. You can seem 1 foot shorter or taller and can appear thin, fat, or in between. You can't change your body type, so you must adopt a form that has the same basic arrangement of limbs. Otherwise, the extent of the illusion is up to you.

The changes wrought by this spell fail to hold up to physical inspection. For example, if you use this spell to add a hat to your outfit, objects pass through the hat, and anyone who touches it would feel nothing or would feel your head and hair. If you use this spell to appear thinner than you are, the hand of someone who reaches out to touch you would bump into you while it was seemingly still in midair.


To discern that you are disguised, a creature can use its action to inspect your appearance and must succeed on an Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC.



2nd Level

Shadow Blade

You weave together threads of shadow to create a sword of solidified gloom in your hand. This magic sword lasts until the spell ends. It counts as a simple melee weapon with which you are proficient. It deals 2d8 psychic damage on a hit and has the finesse, light, and thrown properties (range 20/60). In addition, when you use the sword to attack a target that is in dim light or darkness, you make the attack roll with advantage.

If you drop the weapon or throw it, it dissipates at the end of the turn. Thereafter, while the spell persists, you can use a bonus action to cause the sword to reappear in your hand.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a 3rd- or 4th-level spell slot, the damage increases to 3d8. When you cast it using a 5th- or 6th-level spell slot, the damage increases to 4d8. When you cast it using a spell slot of 7th level or higher, the damage increases to 5d8.



Tasha's Mind Whip

You psychically lash out at one creature you can see within range. The target must make an Intelligence saving throw. On a failed save, the target takes 3d6 psychic damage, and it can’t take a reaction until the end of its next turn. Moreover, on its next turn, it must choose whether it gets a move, an action, or a bonus action; it gets only one of the three. On a successful save, the target takes half as much damage and suffers none of the spell’s other effects.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, you can target one additional creature for each slot level above 2nd. The creatures must be within 30 feet of each other when you target them.


Hold Person

Choose a humanoid that you can see within range. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be paralyzed for the duration. At the end of each of its turns, the target can make another Wisdom saving throw. On a success, the spell ends on the target.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, you can target one additional humanoid for each slot level above 2nd. The humanoids must be within 30 feet of each other when you target them.


Invisibility

A creature you touch becomes invisible until the spell ends. Anything the target is wearing or carrying is invisible as long as it is on the target's person. The spell ends for a target that attacks or casts a spell.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 3rd level or higher, you can target one additional creature for each slot level above 2nd.


Detect Thoughts

For the duration, you can read the thoughts of certain creatures. When you cast the spell and as your action on each turn until the spell ends, you can focus your mind on any one creature that you can see within 30 feet of you. If the creature you choose has an Intelligence of 3 or lower or doesn’t speak any language, the creature is unaffected.


You initially learn the surface thoughts of the creature—what is most on its mind in that moment. As an action, you can either shift your attention to another creature’s thoughts or attempt to probe deeper into the same creature’s mind. If you probe deeper, the target must make a Wisdom saving throw. If it fails, you gain insight into its reasoning (if any), its emotional state, and something that looms large in its mind (such as something it worries over, loves, or hates). If it succeeds, the spell ends. Either way, the target knows that you are probing into its mind, and unless you shift your attention to another creature’s thoughts, the creature can use its action on its turn to make an Intelligence check contested by your Intelligence check; if it succeeds, the spell ends.


Questions verbally directed at the target creature naturally shape the course of its thoughts, so this spell is particularly effective as part of an interrogation.


You can also use this spell to detect the presence of thinking creatures you can’t see. When you cast the spell or as your action during the duration, you can search for thoughts within 30 feet of you. The spell can penetrate barriers, but 2 feet of rock, 2 inches of any metal other than lead, or a thin sheet of lead blocks you. You can’t detect a creature with an Intelligence of 3 or lower or one that doesn’t speak any language.


Once you detect the presence of a creature in this way, you can read its thoughts for the rest of the duration as described above, even if you can’t see it, but it must still be within range.


This spell requires no spell slot or components.


Darkness

Magical darkness spreads from a point you choose within range to fill a 15-foot-radius sphere for the duration. The darkness spreads around corners. A creature with darkvision can't see through this darkness, and nonmagical light can't illuminate it.


If the point you choose is on an object you are holding or one that isn't being worn or carried, the darkness emanates from the object and moves with it. Completely covering the source of the darkness with an opaque object, such as a bowl or a helm, blocks the darkness.


If any of this spell's area overlaps with an area of light created by a spell of 2nd level or lower, the spell that created the light is dispelled.


3rd Level

Major Image

You create the image of an object, a creature, or some other visible phenomenon that is no larger than a 20-foot cube. The image appears at a spot that you can see within range and lasts for the duration. It seems completely real, including sounds, smells, and temperature appropriate to the thing depicted. You can't create sufficient heat or cold to cause damage, a sound loud enough to deal thunder damage or deafen a creature, or a smell that might sicken a creature (like a troglodyte's stench).


As long as you are within range of the illusion, you can use your action to cause the image to move to any other spot within range. As the image changes location, you can alter its appearance so that its movements appear natural for the image. For example, if you create an image of a creature and move it, you can alter the image so that it appears to be walking. Similarly, you can cause the illusion to make different sounds at different times, even making it carry on a conversation, for example.


Physical interaction with the image reveals it to be an illusion, because things can pass through it. A creature that uses its action to examine the image can determine that it is an illusion with a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check against your spell save DC. If a creature discerns the illusion for what it is, the creature can see through the image, and its other sensory qualities become faint to the creature.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 6th level or higher, the spell lasts until dispelled, without requiring your concentration.


Enemies Abound

You reach into the mind of one creature you can see and force it to make an Intelligence saving throw. A creature automatically succeeds if it is immune to being frightened. On a failed save, the target loses the ability to distinguish friend from foe, regarding all creatures it can see as enemies until the spell ends. Each time the target takes damage, it can repeat the saving throw, ending the effect on itself on a success. Whenever the affected creature chooses another creature as a target, it must choose the target at random from among the creatures it can see within range of the attack, spell, or other ability it’s using. If an enemy provokes an opportunity attack from the affected creature, the creature must make that attack if it is able to.


Whenever the affected creature chooses another creature as a target, it must choose the target at random from among the creatures it can see within range of the attack, spell, or other ability it’s using. If an enemy provokes an opportunity attack from the affected creature, the creature must make that attack if it is able to.


Fireball

A bright streak flashes from your pointing finger to a point you choose within range and then blossoms with a low roar into an explosion of flame. Each creature in a 20-foot-radius sphere centered on that point must make a Dexterity saving throw. A target takes 8d6 fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one.


The fire spreads around corners. It ignites flammable objects in the area that aren't being worn or carried.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 4th level or higher, the damage increases by 1d6 for each slot level above 3rd.


4th Level

Banishment

You attempt to send one creature that you can see within range to another plane of existence. The target must succeed on a Charisma saving throw or be banished.


If the target is native to the plane of existence you're on, you banish the target to a harmless demiplane. While there, the target is incapacitated. The target remains there until the spell ends, at which point the target reappears in the space it left or in the nearest unoccupied space if that space is occupied.


If the target is native to a different plane of existence than the one you're on, the target is banished with a faint popping noise, returning to its home plane. If the spell ends before 1 minute has passed, the target reappears in the space it left or in the nearest unoccupied space if that space is occupied. Otherwise, the target doesn't return.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 5th level or higher, you can target one additional creature for each slot level above 4th.


5th Level

Hold Monster

Choose a creature that you can see within range. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be paralyzed for the duration. This spell has no effect on undead. At the end of each of its turns, the target can make another Wisdom saving throw. On a success, the spell ends on the target.


At Higher Levels: When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 6th level or higher, you can target one additional creature for each slot level above 5th. The creatures must be within 30 feet of each other when you target them.

Tools

Dice Set

Aman is proficient with a gaming set, and can add his proficiency bonus to ability checks made to play a game with that set.


Forgery Kit

This small box contains a variety of papers and parchments, pens and inks, seals and sealing wax, gold and silver leaf, and other supplies necessary to create convincing forgeries of physical documents. Aman is proficient with this kit, allowing him to add his proficiency bonus to any ability checks made to create a physical forgery of a document.


Thieves' Tools

This set of tools includes a small file, a set of lock picks, a small mirror mounted on a metal handle, a set of narrow-bladed scissors, and a pair of pliers. Aman is proficiency with these tools, allowing him to add his proficiency bonus to any ability checks made to disarm traps or open locks.


Languages and Communication

Common

Common is a language spoken by most races and creatures in D&D. Aman's native language is Common.


Abyssal

The language of demons. Once part of another language, Primordial, Abyssal deviated over time to become its own distinct language. Aman learned Abyssal during his studies as a shadow sorcerer.


Telepathy (60 ft)

You can speak telepathically to any creature you can see within 60 feet of you. Your telepathic utterances are in a language you know, and the creature understands you only if it knows that language. Your communication doesn’t give the creature the ability to respond to you telepathically.
